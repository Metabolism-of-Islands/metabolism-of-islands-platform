Rate how much you prefer each alternative with respect to each single criterion using the slider below. This page is about the selected criterion ({{ criteria }}). Move the slider for every alternative to reflect relative desirability for this criterion only — higher slider = stronger preference for that alternative related to {{ criteria }}. These local preference scores convert qualitative judgments into numbers that, together with the criterion weight, determine each alternative’s overall score: AHP multiplies each alternative’s score under a criterion by that criterion’s importance, then sums across all criteria. That linkage ensures an alternative can win either by being strong on very important criteria or by being uniformly good across many less-important ones, so make the slider settings reflect how much each alternative truly meets that specific criterion, not an overall judgment.

The relative-importance slider lets you express how much more (or less) important one criterion is compared with the other. Move each slider to scale a criterion’s weight. Behind the scenes these sliders are normalized into a set of weights that sum to 1, so changing one slider redistributes importance across all criteria. Those weights are then used to scale the alternatives’ scores per criterion, meaning a criterion with a larger slider has proportionally greater influence on the final overall ranking.

Sensitivity analysis

Sensitivity analysis shows how stable the decision is when the importance of criteria changes. By moving a slider, you can increase or decrease the importance of a specific criterion and immediately see how the overall rankings respond. As one criterion becomes more important, the system automatically adjusts the importance of the remaining criteria so that the total importance stays balanced. This helps you explore “what-if” scenarios and understand which criteria have the biggest influence on the final result. You can freely play around with these sliders - this will not change your saved preferences and results.
